
            This proposal suggests transforming Tamil Nadu's 14 cooperative sugar mills into advanced bio-refineries by replacing traditional sugarcane cultivation with high-starch industrial sweet potatoes, which grow faster, require less water, and produce more starch for fuel production. The harvested sweet potatoes are processed into ethanol through fermentation, while the remaining waste is converted into biogas and then chemically transformed into Bio-LPG for household cooking. The ethanol is sold to oil companies to generate revenue, and the Bio-LPG is distributed through the Public Distribution System (PDS) to provide six free cooking gas cylinders each year to eligible rural families. The project aims to increase farmers' income through guaranteed pricing and faster payments, reduce government spending on loss-making sugar mills, promote renewable energy, lower carbon emissions, and create a financially self-sustaining system that supports both economic growth and public welfare
